What

Redesigned a wearable magnetic actuation system to drive mucus-clearing airway stent prototypes, improving portability, precision control, and feasibility for future clinical applications.

How

  • Designed 3D assemblies in SolidWorks and fabricated PLA actuation platforms.
  • Developed Arduino-controlled electronics with servo and linear motor control.
  • Validated magnetic field and mucus-clearing performance using MATLAB and ImageJ.
  • Reduced total system weight by 34% (686 g → 450 g) through component optimization.

Why

  • Enhanced patient wearability and portability.
  • Enabled precise multi-axis magnetic field control.
  • Demonstrated successful mucus pumping in benchtop trials.

Ciliary Mucus Pumping Stent

Miniature Robotics (Dong) Lab · May – Aug 2025

Stent Render
Cilia Actuation
Mucus Pumping
Sheep Trachea X-Ray

What

Developed and tested an airway stent embedded with magnetically actuated cilia to enhance mucus clearance in patients with respiratory compromise.

How

  • Fabricated artificial cilia using polymer processing, CAD, and laser cutting.
  • Reduced required actuation field by 60% (to 15 mT) via geometry optimization.
  • Performed dynamic trials in simulated airways and analyzed results with MATLAB/ImageJ.
  • Validated stent delivery and positioning via X-ray in a sheep trachea model.

Why

  • Demonstrated reliable mucus transport under physiological conditions.
  • Confirmed clinical feasibility through phantom and in-vivo testing.
  • Advanced minimally invasive airway therapy development.
